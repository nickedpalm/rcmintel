## How to Choose a Medical Billing Service in Austin

- **Verify their certifications — then verify them again.** CHBME, CPC, CCS, CMRS — these aren't vanity credentials. A biller with a CPC from AAPC has passed a 150-question exam on CPT and ICD-10 coding. Ask which staff hold active certifications, not just whether the company "has certified coders."
- **Ask for specialty-specific experience in Texas Medicaid.** Texas Medicaid (TMHP) has its own quirks — prior auth rules, managed care overlap, and claim submission portals that trip up billers used to other states. If your practice sees Medicaid patients, this matters immediately.
- **Demand clean claim rate and days-in-AR benchmarks from current clients.** Industry standard: clean claim rates above 95%, AR days below 35. Any company that can't (or won't) share these numbers for accounts similar to yours is telling you something.
- **Understand what "denial management" actually means in their workflow.** Some shops file the claim and call it done. Others work every denial to resolution. Ask specifically: what's their denial follow-up SLA, and how do they handle underpayments versus outright rejections?
- **Get clarity on contract terms before you sign.** Month-to-month vs. annual, termination notice windows, and data portability (you own your patient data — make sure the contract says so explicitly).

> **Pro Tip:** Austin has a dense concentration of VC-backed direct primary care and concierge practices, which means some billing companies here have built specific expertise in hybrid billing models — insurance plus membership fees. If your practice runs any kind of membership layer, ask whether they've billed that combination before.

## What to Expect

Pricing for medical billing services in Austin runs $500–$5,000 per month depending on practice size, specialty, and service scope — most small-to-mid practices land in the $1,000–$2,500 range on a percentage-of-collections model (typically 4–8%). Expect a 30–60 day onboarding ramp before you see normalized AR metrics, and plan for at least one full billing cycle before meaningful performance data surfaces.

> **Reality Check:** The lowest-percentage bid is almost never the best deal. A 4% rate sounds better than 7% until you realize the cheaper company's clean claim rate is 88% and you're eating the difference in rework, delayed payments, and staff time chasing denials. Model the math on your actual monthly collections before you anchor to the headline rate.

## Local Market Overview

Austin's healthcare sector has grown faster than almost any other major metro — the city added tens of thousands of residents in the last decade, and new independent practices, urgent care clinics, and specialty groups have followed. That growth means local billers are generally well-versed in high-volume credentialing and payer contracting with BlueCross BlueShield of Texas, Aetna, and the major Central Texas managed care networks, but it also means demand for experienced billing staff is tight — which is exactly why outsourcing to a credentialed firm often makes more operational sense here than hiring in-house.
