## How to Choose a Medical Billing Service in Jackson

- **Verify credentials before the first call.** Look for CHBME, CPC, or CCS designations — these aren't vanity certs. A Certified Healthcare Billing and Management Executive (HBMA) has passed a rigorous competency exam. A CPC (AAPC) or CCS (AHIMA) credential means they know how to code correctly the first time, which directly affects your denial rate.
- **Ask for Mississippi Medicaid experience specifically.** Mississippi has one of the highest Medicaid enrollment rates in the country — over 20% of the population. If a billing service can't speak fluently about Mississippi Division of Medicaid (DOM) billing quirks, payer edits, and prior auth workflows, they're not the right fit for a Jackson practice.
- **Demand benchmark metrics, not promises.** Any reputable service should hand you their current portfolio averages: clean claim rate (target: 95%+), days in AR (target: under 35), and net collection ratio (target: 95%+). If they hedge on sharing these numbers, walk.
- **Understand who actually touches your claims.** Some "billing companies" are staffing agencies with offshore coders and a US-based account manager who reads you dashboards. Others have in-house credentialed staff. Know which model you're buying before you sign.
- **Check contract terms for exit clauses.** A 12-month lock-in with a 90-day termination notice is not unusual — but it's also leverage that disappears the moment you sign. Negotiate for a 30-day out after the first 90 days if performance benchmarks aren't met.

> **Pro Tip:** Ask your malpractice carrier or a local MGMA chapter contact for a referral. Mississippi chapter members often have firsthand experience with regional billing services and will tell you exactly who to avoid — information that never shows up in a Google review.

## What to Expect

Monthly fees for medical billing services in Jackson typically run $500–$2,500 for small to mid-size practices (under 3 providers), and can reach $5,000 or more for larger multi-specialty groups or practices with high-complexity coding needs like interventional pain or neurology. Most contracts are structured as a percentage of collections (typically 4–8%) rather than a flat fee, which aligns incentives but also means your bill grows as you grow.

> **Reality Check:** The cheapest billing service almost always costs you more. A service charging 4% but running a 78% clean claim rate will cost you more in write-offs and resubmission labor than one charging 7% with a 96% clean claim rate. Run the math on projected collections, not the percentage alone.
