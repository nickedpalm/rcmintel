## How to Choose a Medical Billing Service in Burlington

- **Verify credentials before anything else.** Ask for CHBME, CPC, or CCS certification numbers and look them up directly on HBMA, AAPC, or AHIMA's verification portals. A company that can't produce these in 24 hours is not a company you want near your AR.
- **Ask specifically about Vermont Medicaid (Green Mountain Care).** Vermont's Medicaid program has its own quirks around fee schedules and prior auth requirements. A billing service that handles high volumes of Green Mountain Care claims is categorically different from one that mostly works Medicare — make sure they can speak to it specifically.
- **Request a clean claim rate and days-in-AR benchmark.** Any competent shop tracks these. Industry baseline is 95%+ clean claims on first submission and under 35 days in AR. If they hedge on sharing numbers, that's your answer.
- **Understand the denial management workflow.** Denials happen. What matters is how fast they're worked. Ask: who touches a denial, within how many days, and what's the appeal rate? A vague answer here usually means denials are sitting in a queue.
- **Get a reference from a similarly-sized practice in Vermont or New Hampshire.** Burlington's market is small enough that a good biller will have local references. A practice manager at a 3-physician family medicine group in Essex or South Burlington has way more useful intel than a testimonial on a website.

> **Pro Tip:** Vermont has a higher-than-average share of ACO and value-based contracts through OneCare Vermont. If your practice participates in OneCare, specifically ask whether the billing service has experience with shared savings reconciliation and attributed patient reporting — this is a non-trivial wrinkle that generic billers routinely miss.

## What to Expect

Monthly fees for a full-service medical billing engagement in Burlington typically run $500–$5,000 depending on practice size, specialty, and claim volume — most small-to-mid-sized practices land in the $1,200–$2,500 range. Onboarding usually takes 2–4 weeks for credentialing and EHR integration, with the first clean billing cycle following in week 5 or 6. Expect 30–60 days before you have meaningful performance data.

> **Reality Check:** Percentage-of-collections pricing (typically 4–9%) sounds appealing because it aligns incentives, but it can end up more expensive than flat-fee arrangements for high-volume, low-complexity practices. Run the math against your actual monthly collections before signing. A service quoting 7% on a $60K/month practice is charging $4,200 — a flat $2,000/month might be a better deal with identical service quality.

## Local Market Overview

Burlington's healthcare economy is anchored by the University of Vermont Medical Center and a dense cluster of independent specialty and primary care practices — which means billing complexity runs the gamut from straightforward fee-for-service to multi-payer academic billing arrangements. The city's proximity to the Canadian border also generates a niche volume of cross-border patient encounters that require careful payer identification and occasionally manual claims handling that offshore billing shops routinely fumble.
