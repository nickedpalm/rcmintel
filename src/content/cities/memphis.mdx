This directory exists so you don't have to learn that lesson the expensive way.

## How to Choose a Medical Billing Service in Memphis

- **Verify credentials before anything else.** Ask for CHBME, CPC, or CCS certifications upfront. Any firm worth hiring will hand you a staff credentialing sheet without blinking. If they hedge, that tells you everything.
- **Ask specifically about Tennessee Medicaid (TennCare) experience.** TennCare MCO billing — with BlueCare Tennessee, UnitedHealthcare Community Plan, and Amerigroup all operating in Shelby County — has its own denial quirks. A national firm that's never touched a TennCare remit will cost you money before they save you any.
- **Get a sample denial report from a current client.** Clean claim rate and days-in-AR numbers are easy to quote. A real denial analysis report — with root causes and resolution timelines — shows whether they actually manage the back end or just submit and pray.
- **Understand who handles your account day-to-day.** National firms often assign high-volume accounts to offshore teams without disclosing it. Ask directly: where are the coders located, and what's the escalation path when a payer disputes a claim?
- **Check for specialty-specific experience.** Memphis has a significant concentration of federally qualified health centers (FQHCs) and large hospital-affiliated physician groups. If your practice does anything outside primary care — oncology, behavioral health, high-complexity surgery — make sure their coding team has done it before.

> **Pro Tip:** Request a 90-day performance review clause in your contract. Any firm confident in their numbers will agree to it. If they push back, that's a red flag worth taking seriously.

## What to Expect

Most Memphis practices pay between $500 and $2,500 per month for a small-to-midsize primary care panel, with high-volume specialty groups running $3,000–$5,000 or more depending on claim complexity and add-on services like credentialing or patient payment portal management. Onboarding typically takes two to four weeks — expect a practice analysis, EHR access setup, and a parallel billing period before full handoff.

> **Reality Check:** The "percentage of collections" pricing model sounds low-risk but can quietly cost more than a flat fee once your volume scales. Get the math done on both structures before you sign. A 6% rate on $80K monthly collections is $4,800 — often higher than a flat-fee equivalent for the same workload.

## Local Market Overview

Memphis's healthcare economy punches well above its weight — Methodist Le Bonheur, Baptist Memorial, and St. Francis together employ thousands of affiliated physicians, and the downstream effect is a dense market of independent and group practices competing for the same payer contracts. That density means Memphis billers tend to have strong commercial payer relationships with BlueCross BlueShield of Tennessee and Cigna, but it also means the market has no shortage of firms willing to undercut on price and overdeliver on promises — which is exactly why vetting credentials and pulling real performance data matters more here than in a smaller metro.
