## How to Choose a Medical Billing Service in Stamford

- **Verify certifications before the first call.** Look for CHBME, CPC, or CCS credentials — not just "certified billers." Connecticut has a high concentration of commercial payers (Aetna, Cigna, and UnitedHealthcare all have major operations in Fairfield County), so coding depth matters more here than in markets dominated by Medicare.
- **Ask specifically about Connecticut Medicaid (HUSKY Health) experience.** CT's Medicaid MCO structure is unusual — four managed care plans administer benefits, each with slightly different claim submission requirements. A firm that only knows fee-for-service Medicare will leave money on the table.
- **Demand a clean claim rate and days-in-AR benchmark upfront.** Any reputable service will share these numbers. Target: 95%+ clean claim rate, sub-30-day AR. If they hedge on either, walk.
- **Confirm EHR compatibility before signing anything.** Fairfield County practices run everything from Epic and athenahealth to niche specialty platforms. Integration failure is the single biggest source of billing delays in year one.
- **Get contract terms in plain language.** Watch for evergreen clauses and early-termination fees — a common trap with larger national firms who sign Stamford practices and then route work to offshore teams.

> **Pro Tip:** Stamford sits close to the [New York](/locations/new-york/new-york/) border, so many practices here have patients covered by New York-based employer plans. Ask candidates directly how they handle out-of-state commercial payer contracts and cross-state credentialing discrepancies.

## What to Expect

Most full-service medical billing contracts for a Stamford practice run $500–$5,000 per month, typically structured as a percentage of collections (5–10%) or a flat per-claim fee depending on volume. Onboarding takes two to four weeks — plan for a parallel-run period where your old process and the new firm run simultaneously before you fully cut over. Reporting cadence should be monthly at minimum, with real-time dashboard access standard from any firm worth hiring in 2026.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake practices make is optimizing for the lowest percentage rate. A firm charging 6% with a 91% clean claim rate will cost you more than one charging 9% with a 97% rate — the math on denied and resubmitted claims always wins. Run the numbers on collection yield, not the headline fee.
