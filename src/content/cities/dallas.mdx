## How to Choose a Medical Billing Service in Dallas

- **Verify credentials before the demo.** Look for CHBME, CPC, or CCS designations on the actual coders handling your claims — not just the company's marketing page. Texas has no state-level licensing for medical billers, so credentials from HBMA, AAPC, or AHIMA are the only third-party validation you'll get.
- **Ask for specialty-specific denial rates.** A billing company crushing it for family medicine may be underwater on orthopedics or behavioral health. Dallas has a dense specialist market — cardiology, oncology, and pain management practices especially need billers who know their payer mix cold.
- **Get a clean claim rate benchmark in writing.** Industry standard is 95%+. If a vendor won't give you their current clean claim rate for a practice in your specialty, that's the answer.
- **Understand their Texas Medicaid experience.** Texas Medicaid (TMHP) has its own quirks — prior auth rules, managed care plan carve-outs, and a notoriously complex portal. Ask specifically how many Texas Medicaid providers they currently bill for.
- **Check their payer contract negotiation stance.** Some billing companies will renegotiate your fee schedules with commercial payers as part of onboarding; others explicitly won't touch it. In the DFW market where BCBS of Texas and UnitedHealthcare dominate, this can be worth thousands annually.

> **Pro Tip:** Ask for a 90-day performance report from a current client in your specialty before signing. Any reputable billing company operating in Dallas has this data. If they hesitate, walk.

## What to Expect

Medical billing services in Dallas typically run $500–$5,000 per month depending on practice size, specialty, and whether you're outsourcing coding, billing, or the full revenue cycle. Most price as a percentage of collections (4–9% is common) rather than a flat fee, which aligns incentives — they only get paid when you do. Expect a 30–60 day onboarding period before you see clean performance data, and build 90 days into any realistic evaluation window.

> **Reality Check:** The cheapest percentage rate isn't the best deal. A biller charging 5% with a 91% clean claim rate will cost you more in write-offs and rework than one charging 7% with a 97% rate. Model the math on your actual monthly collections before negotiating on rate alone.

## Local Market Overview

Dallas sits at the intersection of a massive employer-sponsored insurance market and one of the most competitive independent practice environments in Texas — the city added over 3,000 net new healthcare providers between 2020 and 2024, most of them small-to-mid-size practices that need billing support from day one. The DFW payer landscape is dominated by BCBS of Texas, Aetna, and Cigna, with Texas Medicaid adding complexity for any practice serving the city's substantial Medicaid-eligible population. Billers with deep DFW experience know these contracts and their edge cases; ones parachuting in from out of state often don't.
