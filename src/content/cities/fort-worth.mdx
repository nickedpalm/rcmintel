## How to Choose a Medical Billing Service in Fort Worth

- **Verify credentials before anything else.** Ask for proof of CHBME, CPC, or CCS certification — not just "years of experience." HBMA and AAPC both have public lookup tools. Any firm that hedges on this is telling you something.

- **Ask about Texas Medicaid STAR specifically.** Texas routes Medicaid through managed care organizations — Molina, UnitedHealthcare Community, BCBS of Texas — each with its own prior auth rules and claim formatting requirements. A billing service that's never navigated STAR MCO denials will cost you money before you realize the problem.

- **Demand clean claim rate benchmarks.** Industry standard is 95% or above. Ask what theirs is. If they don't track it or won't share it, that's your answer.

- **Find out who's actually doing the work.** Fort Worth has several "billing companies" that are one-person operations with a polished website and a contractor network they don't fully supervise. That's not automatically disqualifying — but you need accountability baked into the contract.

- **Match on specialty.** A firm that codes nothing but orthopedics is the wrong choice for a behavioral health or addiction medicine practice. Texas Medicaid has documentation requirements that vary sharply by specialty — your biller needs to know yours cold before the first claim goes out.

> **Pro Tip:** Ask any candidate what their average days-in-AR looks like for a practice your size and specialty. A healthy number is under 30. If they quote you 45 or hedge with "it depends," your collections will drag for months while you figure out why.

## What to Expect

Pricing in Fort Worth runs $500–$5,000/month depending on specialty, claim volume, and scope of service — most small-to-mid practices land in the $800–$2,000 range. Full-service firms typically charge 4–8% of net collections; flat-rate models are more common for high-volume primary care. Expect 2–4 weeks for onboarding and 30–60 days before AR metrics meaningfully improve.

> **Reality Check:** The cheapest option almost always means a percentage-only model with no performance guarantees and zero incentive to chase difficult denials. A $600/month service that lets 15% of claims go unpaid is more expensive than a $1,800/month service collecting 97%. Run the math on your monthly collections volume before defaulting to the lowest quote.
