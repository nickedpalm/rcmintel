## How to Choose a Medical Billing Service in Los Angeles

- **Verify credentials before anything else.** Look for CHBME, CPC, or CCS certification on staff — not just on the company's homepage, but ask for the individual credentialer names and verify them directly through HBMA or AAPC's directories. Los Angeles has no shortage of billing shops staffed by people with a weekend course and a QuickBooks login.
- **Ask for specialty-specific experience.** LA's provider mix skews heavily toward concierge medicine, cash-pay aesthetics, and capitated IPA arrangements — billing rules for each are meaningfully different. A service that crushes it for a fee-for-service orthopedic group may be completely lost inside a delegated risk contract.
- **Demand payer-mix transparency.** California's Medi-Cal managed care landscape is one of the most complex in the country, with 24 separate plans under Covered California and a patchwork of county-specific Medi-Cal DMC-ODS contracts. If a billing service can't name the specific plans they regularly work with, that's a red flag.
- **Request a 90-day performance snapshot.** Before signing, ask for a real client's clean claim rate, days in AR, and net collection ratio — benchmarks, not just testimonials. Industry standard is a clean claim rate above 95% and days in AR under 35. If they balk at sharing this, they're hiding something.
- **Check for California AB 1305 and HIPAA compliance posture.** California's data privacy laws layer on top of HIPAA. Your billing partner handles PHI daily — confirm they carry cyber liability insurance and can articulate their breach response protocol.

> **Pro Tip:** Ask specifically whether the service uses a clearinghouse that scrubs against California's Medi-Cal claim edits before submission. A lot of national billing services skip this step and then bill their time to work the denials they created.

## What to Expect

Monthly fees for a full-service medical billing partner in Los Angeles typically land between $500 and $5,000, though most mid-sized practices in the $800K–$2M annual collections range end up around 6–8% of net collections. Most services take 30–60 days to fully onboard and start showing meaningful AR improvement, with steady-state performance benchmarks visible by month three.

> **Reality Check:** Flat-rate pricing sounds attractive but often hides the catch — services that charge a low flat fee tend to quietly deprioritize older claims and difficult denials because they don't get paid more to fight them. Percentage-of-collections aligns incentives correctly: they only win when you win.
