## How to Choose a Medical Billing Service in Long Beach

- **Verify California-specific payer knowledge.** Medi-Cal managed care plans (LA Care, Molina, Health Net) have reimbursement quirks that out-of-state billing shops routinely fumble. Ask any candidate to describe how they handle Medi-Cal TAR (Treatment Authorization Request) denials — if they pause, that's your answer.
- **Demand credential transparency.** Look for CHBME, CPC, or CCS designations on the staff actually working your account — not just on the owner's bio page. AAPC and AHIMA both have public credential verification tools. Use them.
- **Ask for specialty-specific clean claim rates.** A 95% clean claim rate for primary care means nothing if you're running a pain management or mental health practice. Long Beach has a dense concentration of specialty clinics along the 710 corridor — your biller should have experience with your exact specialty codes.
- **Get a denial management SLA in writing.** The best services commit to resubmitting denials within 48-72 hours and give you a monthly denial reason code breakdown. If they can't show you last quarter's top five denial codes for a comparable client, they're not tracking it closely enough.
- **Check for Cloudflare-era data security basics.** Patient data handling in California means HIPAA plus CMIA (Confidentiality of Medical Information Act). Your BAA should reference both. Non-California billers often miss the CMIA requirement entirely.

> **Pro Tip:** Long Beach Memorial and CHOC have referral relationships with several local billing groups. That's not a red flag — but it does mean some shops are optimized for hospital-adjacent practices, not independent clinics. If you're a solo-practice internist or a small group, ask directly whether they work with practices your size.

## What to Expect

Most Long Beach practices pay between $500 and $2,500 per month for a full-service medical billing engagement — the high end of the $500-5,000 range is typically reserved for high-volume specialty groups or practices requiring credentialing services bundled in. Onboarding takes two to four weeks: expect a practice analysis, EHR integration setup, and a claims audit of your last 90 days before they submit anything new.

> **Reality Check:** Percentage-of-collections pricing sounds low-risk until you realize it creates an incentive to chase easy claims and abandon the hard denials. A 7% fee on $80,000/month in collections is $5,600 — often more expensive than a flat-rate service, and with worse AR follow-through. Get a flat-rate quote before you anchor on percentage pricing.
