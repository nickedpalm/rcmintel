## How to Choose a Medical Billing Service in Charleston

- **Verify credentials before the sales call ends.** CHBME, CPC, and CCS aren't vanity acronyms — they're the difference between someone who passed an exam and someone who knows how South Carolina Medicaid's prior authorization quirks differ from CMS. Ask which staff hold them and for how many of your claim types.

- **Match the service to your specialty.** A biller who's excellent with family medicine will get wrecked by orthopedic or behavioral health coding. Charleston's healthcare landscape is anchored by MUSC Health and a growing network of independent specialty practices — make sure your biller has handled your exact CPT/ICD-10 territory.

- **Demand a clean claim rate benchmark upfront.** Any serious shop should be targeting 95%+ first-pass acceptance. If they can't tell you their current rate, that number is bad and they know it.

- **Ask about South Carolina Medicaid specifically.** SC Healthy Connections Medicaid has its own portal, its own quirks, and its own denial patterns. National firms sometimes treat it like a rounding error. A Charleston-based or SC-familiar biller will not.

- **Get days-in-AR in writing as a performance metric.** Industry standard is under 40 days. If a vendor won't commit to a benchmark in your contract, they're protecting themselves, not you.

> **Pro Tip:** Charleston's tight-knit medical community means reputation travels fast. Ask for two or three references from practices in your specialty — and actually call them. A 10-minute conversation with a fellow practice manager is worth more than any sales deck.

## What to Expect

Medical billing services in Charleston typically run $500–$5,000 per month depending on practice size, specialty complexity, and claim volume — with most mid-sized independent practices landing in the $1,200–$2,500 range. Some services charge a flat monthly fee; others take a percentage of collections (typically 4–9%). Onboarding runs two to four weeks for a clean practice transition; if you're migrating mid-year from a broken system or a prior biller's backlog, budget six to eight weeks before you're seeing clean reporting.

> **Reality Check:** The percentage-of-collections model sounds aligned with your interests, but it can quietly discourage billers from chasing smaller or messier claims. If your practice has a lot of low-dollar high-complexity encounters, a flat fee with explicit denial follow-up SLAs in the contract will serve you better.

Charleston's healthcare economy punches well above its weight for a city of 150,000 — MUSC's academic medical center, a dense corridor of specialist practices along the I-526 belt, and a steady influx of new residents from the Southeast expansion have created strong demand for billing services that understand both high-volume institutional workflows and the tight margins of independent practice. The city's growth also means a lot of newly opened practices entering the market without billing infrastructure in place, which is exactly the scenario where a credentialed local service — rather than a national clearinghouse with a 1-800 number — pays for itself in the first quarter.
