## How to Choose a Medical Billing Service in Raleigh

- **Verify credentials before the first call.** Look for CHBME, CPC, or CCS designations — these aren't vanity certificates. A CHBME (from HBMA) means the company has cleared a rigorous management-level review. A CPC (AAPC) or CCS (AHIMA) on the coding side means the person actually touching your charts passed a standardized exam. Ask to see current certificates, not just logos on a website.
- **Ask for specialty-specific experience.** A service that does great work for primary care may be a disaster for dermatology or ortho. North Carolina's payer mix skews heavily toward BCBS of NC (the dominant commercial carrier in the state), so ask explicitly: how many claims do you process through BCBS NC monthly, and what's your clean claim rate with them?
- **Get benchmark numbers upfront.** Any service worth hiring should volunteer their days-in-AR, net collection ratio, and first-pass resolution rate. If they hedge or give you a range so wide it's meaningless ("we typically see 95–99%"), that's a flag. Push for actual client data.
- **Understand the contract exit terms.** Some Raleigh-area services lock you into 12-month agreements with 90-day notice clauses. If you're a new practice or switching vendors after a bad experience, a 60-day out is the standard you should negotiate for.
- **Check for EHR compatibility.** RTP-area practices tend to run Epic, athenahealth, or Modernizing Medicine. Confirm your biller has worked inside your exact system — not just "we integrate with most EHRs" — before you sign anything.

> **Pro Tip:** Ask your prospective billing service for a sample monthly report before you contract. If they can't produce a clean AR aging report, a denial reason code breakdown, and a payer mix summary on demand, they're not running the back office you need.

## What to Expect

Medical billing services in Raleigh typically charge a percentage of collections — usually 4–9% for most specialties — or a flat monthly fee that lands between $500 and $5,000 depending on your practice size and claim volume. Onboarding takes 2–4 weeks for a clean handoff; rushed transitions are where errors compound. Expect your first clean-claim-rate report within 30–45 days of go-live.

> **Reality Check:** The cheapest percentage fee usually isn't the cheapest outcome. A service charging 5% with a 78% net collection ratio will cost you more than one charging 7% with a 94% rate. Always run the math on collected revenue, not just the fee percentage — that's where practices get burned.
